- **Name**: Soy sauce (Ganjang)
- **Other Names**: 간장
- **Type**: Condiment

## Description

Ganjang is a dark, salty, and umami-rich sauce made from fermented soybeans, wheat, and salt. It is a staple seasoning in Korean cuisine, used in a wide variety of dishes for its depth of flavor and color. Korean soy sauce comes in several varieties, including regular and soup soy sauce.

## How to use

- As a seasoning for stir-fries, braises, and marinades
- In dipping sauces for dumplings and vegetables
- Added to soups and stews for umami and color
- Used in pickling and preserving

## Substitutions

If ganjang is unavailable, Japanese shoyu or Chinese light soy sauce can be used, though the flavor may differ. For gluten-free needs, use tamari.

## Storage

Store soy sauce in an airtight container in the refrigerator or a cool, dark place. It will keep for several months due to its high salt content.
